  <h3>Screening Test Syllabus</h3>
   <p>
Candidates applying to the MTech Industrial AI  program will have to appear in the online screening test. The screening test will be in English and consists of objective type multiple choice questions (MCQs), multiple select questions (MSQs) and numerical answer type questions (NAT). Detailed instructions will be provided in the question paper provided during the exam. Candidates must follow the guidelines for the screening test sent via email.
  </p>

<Curriculum>
  <Semester title="Core Courses">
    <Course courseName="Core 1: Mathematical Foundations for Data Science">
      <CourseContent>
        Basics of Data Science, Linear Algebra for Data Science, Probability, Statistics and Random Processes for Data Science, Optimization for Data Science
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Core 2: Applied Time Series Analysis">
      <CourseContent>
        Introduction to TimeSeries – Analysis, Partial Auto- Correlation Function, Power Spectrum, Basics and Design of filters for data cleaning and preprocessingÍ3⁄4 Kalman filterÍ3⁄4 Applications to process data
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Core 3: Multivariate Data Analysis">
      <CourseContent>
        Introduction to multivariate data analytics and machine learning. Function approximation and classification problems Multivariate Data Analytics: Principal Component Analysis, Kernel Principal Components, Generalized Principal Component Analysis
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Core 4: Machine Learning and its applications">
      <CourseContent>
        Several clustering techniques, Lasso and elastic net SVM, SVR, Decisiontrees, Random forests
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Core 5: Applied Deep Learning">
      <CourseContent>
        Deep Learning: Neural networks basics, Autoencoders, layer-wise learning, deep networks, convolution neural networks, recurrent neural networks, advanced learning algorithms
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Core 6: Online and Reinforcement Learning">
      <CourseContent>
        Introduction to reinforcement learning, value functions and Q-learning, SARSA, RL with function approximation, exploration/exploitation, batch reinforcement learning, online learning, multi-arm bandits
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
  </Semester>

  <Semester title="Labs">
    <Course courseName="Lab 1: Industrial AI Laboratory">
      <CourseContent>
        Hands-on training in data analysis algorithms using Python
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Lab 2: Industrial AI at Scale Laboratory">
      <CourseContent>
        Hands-on training in Big Data - Practical aspects of analytics at large scale, i.e., big data with concepts spanning hardware, systems and software using cloud
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
  </Semester>

  <Semester title="Electives">
    <Course courseName="Elective 1: AI in predictive Maintenance, Reliability and warranty">
      <CourseContent>
        Predictive Maintenance, Failure detection and diagnosis, Benchmarking,Forecasting and recommendations
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Elective 2: AI in process and logistic optimization">
      <CourseContent>
        Process Improvement, Multivariable Optimizing Control, Systems Engineering 1D/ FEM models/ Digital Twins, Supply Chain Management, Computer vision forRetail Stores related (Slip and trip of people, Self- checkout loss in retail stores, Smart docks etc), Smart City
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
    <Course courseName="Elective 3: Industrial Vision AI">
      <CourseContent>
        Introduction of various machine learning models that are needed for solving computer vision problems – Probability, Machine Learning models and inference, Graphical models, Image pre-processing, Multi-view geometry and Models for vision
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
  </Semester>

  <Semester title="Projects">
    <Course courseName="Project I, II, III">
      <CourseContent>
        The project starts in Term 4 and Ends in Term 6.

        A single MTech project will be carried out in three stages (Projects I, II, and III) The minimum duration of the project is 10 months.

        Project work done by the candidate will be defined by them with the consultation of coordinators that includes scope, objective, and data. IITM will review the proposed project work and approve the projects for feasibility, timeline, and deliverables as a part of the MTech project.

        IITM will do intermediate reviews of the project work on agreed milestones and provide feedback to do course correction.

        Once project work is completed by the candidate within stipulated agreed timelines, IITM shall evaluate and provide an appropriate grade for project work.

        In case guidance/facilities are required from IITM for the project, it will be covered under a separate agreement on a case-by-case basis with the concerned faculty.
      </CourseContent>
    </Course>
  </Semester>
</Curriculum>
